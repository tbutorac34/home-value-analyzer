# Product Positioning

## One-Liner
Redfin and Zillow are listing platforms. We're a buying intelligence platform.

## The Problem
Home buyers face an information asymmetry. Listing platforms show you data — price, photos, square footage — but don't tell you what it means. Is this home a good deal? Is the seller motivated? Is the kitchen actually updated or just well-photographed? Should you offer asking price or 10% below?

Buyers are making $300K-700K decisions with the same level of analysis they'd give a restaurant on Yelp.

## What We Do Differently

### 1. We Score Every Property (Deal Finder)
**Them:** Here's a listing. Figure it out.
**Us:** This is a Grade B deal. Score 67/100. Three price drops totaling 12%, seller has been on market 2x longer than average, priced 8% below comparable sales. Here's why.

### 2. We Detect Seller Motivation
**Them:** "Price Reduced" badge.
**Us:** This property was listed at $560K in October, dropped to $540K in November, relisted at $519K in January after a deal fell through. The seller is motivated — our analysis suggests they'd accept $490-500K.

### 3. We Adjust for What Actually Matters
**Them:** Zestimate says $450K (black box, no explanation).
**Us:** Based on 8 comparable sales, adjusted for the finished basement (+$25K), new roof (+$4.8K, installed 2022), and granite kitchen (+$4K), this home's fair value is $462K. Here are the comps and the math.

### 4. We Analyze What You Can't See in a Listing
**Them:** 37 beautiful photos.
**Us:** AI analysis: kitchen is builder-grade (score 5/10), bathrooms were updated (8/10), flooring is LVP not hardwood, overall condition 6.5/10. The listing description says "stunning" but the data says "average."

### 5. Community Intelligence
**Them:** (nothing — no way for buyers to share notes)
**Us:** 3 other buyers commented on this property: "Kitchen photos are misleading — cabinets are original." "Street gets school bus traffic every morning." "Neighbor said seller is relocating for work."

### 6. We're Built for Buyers, Not Agents
**Them:** Make money from agent advertising and referrals. Incentive is to get you to transact.
**Us:** Make money when you make a smarter decision. We'll tell you a home is overpriced. We'll tell you to walk away. Our incentives are aligned with yours.

## Who We're For

### Primary: First-Time and Move-Up Buyers
- Ages 28-45
- Buying in the $250K-750K range
- Data-comfortable but not real estate experts
- Want confidence that they're not overpaying
- Don't fully trust their agent's advice (agent wants a quick close)

### Secondary: Real Estate Investors
- Buy 1-5 properties per year
- Need deal flow and comp analysis
- Will pay more for better data
- Care about $/sqft, cap rates, renovation ROI

### Not For (Yet)
- Sellers (we're buyer-first)
- Agents (they're the incumbent we're disrupting)
- Luxury market (>$1.5M, too unique for automated analysis)

## The Data Moat

Two things competitors can't easily replicate:

1. **Community comments** — user-generated insights on specific properties. This is ours if we build the community first. Zillow/Redfin won't add this because it threatens their agent relationships (agents don't want public comments saying "this home is overpriced").

2. **Feature-adjusted comp analysis** — we have structured data on basement condition, kitchen quality, roof age, etc. extracted from descriptions AND (soon) AI photo analysis. Zillow/Redfin have the raw data but don't surface it in their estimates this way.
